Sherlock's Life

Who is Sherlock Holmes? It's hard to find anyone who hasn't heard of him, but it's equally as difficult to find two people who have exactly the same interpretation. Sherlock Holmes is fictional. Let's get that straight once and for all. Sherlock Holmes was the creation of Sir Arthur Conan Doyle (1859-1930). Doyle was an M.D., and not unsuccessful, but he preferred writing, and eventually abandoned his medical career. He had sold a few short stories when, in 1886, he decided to write a detective story.

Birthplace

Holmes was said to be born in 1854 in England. While the exact location isn't specified, his adventures primarily take place in London.

Family

Holmes has an older brother, Mycroft Holmes, who is also exceptionally intelligent and works for the British government.

221B Baker Street

Sherlock Holmes resided at 221B Baker Street, a fictional location in London that has become synonymous with detective stories. Today, 221B Baker Street has been transformed into the Sherlock Holmes Museum, complete with Victorian furnishings and artifacts related to Holmes' cases.

This site is a tribute to the detective who captured my heart with his sharp mind, thrilling adventures, and unforgettable mysteries. Whether you're already a fan or just curious, I hope you'll enjoy exploring the world of Sherlock Holmes with me.
